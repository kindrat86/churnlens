How It Works

  1. Export a CSV from the target's billing system (Stripe, Chargebee, Recurly, or any system that can produce a subscription history with customer ID, date, amount, and status).
  2. Upload it to Churn Lens — the tool accepts standard subscription export formats and auto-detects the column mapping.
  3. Get the report — within minutes, Churn Lens produces a buyer-side risk report with all six assessment dimensions, benchmark comparisons, and a recommended action for the deal.

Example: Red Flags the Assessment Catches

Here are three real signals Churn Lens flagged in recent uploads:

  • The "Stable MRR" that was actually declining: Target showed $40K MRR flat over 12 months. Churn Lens computed the churn-adjusted projection: $31K MRR by month 18 without new sales. The flat MRR was entirely growth-masked churn (Trick #7).
  • The "diversified" 200-customer base: HHI came back at 3,800. The top 3 customers (a law firm, a university, and a government agency) accounted for 51% of MRR. Two had expiring contracts within 6 months. The deal was re-priced with a 25% concentration discount.
  • The 90% NRR that hid 40% contraction: Target reported 90% NRR. Churn Lens found expansion revenue of 15% (from upsells) and contraction of 40% (from downgrades) — the net was 90% but the contraction was a leading indicator of value erosion that wouldn't show up in the headline number.
