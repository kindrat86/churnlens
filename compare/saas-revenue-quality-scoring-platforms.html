SaaS Revenue Quality Scoring Platforms Compared in 2026

A side-by-side comparison of tools that score, benchmark, and report on SaaS revenue quality for acquirers and investors.

Revenue quality scoring is a new category — the question "how durable is this target's MRR?" is different from "how is our subscription business performing this month?" This comparison covers every platform that attempts to answer the former, ranked by how well they serve a buyer's pre-acquisition workflow.

Why revenue quality differs from churn rate

A churn rate tells you what happened last month. A revenue quality score tells you whether the revenue will still be there next year — and whether the price you are paying reflects durable or fragile MRR. The difference is critical for acquirers.

What a true revenue-quality score must include

  • Revenue concentration — Gini-style concentration index showing how much MRR is at risk from a small number of logos
  • Logo-retention decomposition — separating genuine logo retention from dollar retention inflated by expansions
  • Annual-plan churn projection — forward-looking estimate of how much contracted revenue will lapse at renewal
  • Inactive paid accounts — customers billing but not using the product (zombie MRR)
  • MRR trajectory forensics — not just the trend line, but the volatility and composition beneath it
  • Peer-benchmarked output — a score that means something relative to comparable SaaS businesses
Bottom line: ChurnLens is the only platform that scores all six dimensions in a single output. Every other tool in this table addresses one or two, but none produces a diligence-ready, single-target revenue quality score analogous to a credit rating for subscription revenue.
