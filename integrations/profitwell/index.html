The dual-workflow: ProfitWell for operations, ChurnLens for diligence

ProfitWell and ChurnLens serve complementary roles in a SaaS deal lifecycle. ProfitWell is the operator's platform — it tracks MRR, churn, and subscription metrics from a live billing integration for the team running the business day-to-day. ChurnLens is the buyer's platform — it reconstructs those same metrics from a data-room export to verify whether the numbers ProfitWell would show are actually accurate, before capital changes hands. The two tools do not compete; they operate at different stages and answer different questions.

The practical workflow for an acquirer is: request a ProfitWell export (or the equivalent revenue-ledger CSV) from the seller's data room, run it through ChurnLens's buyer-side analysis, and use the reconstructed metrics to verify the seller's reported figures. If the target is already on ProfitWell, keeping it post-close gives you operational continuity. The pre-close ChurnLens run is what protects the purchase price; the post-close ProfitWell subscription is what runs the business.

A common failure mode in SaaS acquisitions is trusting the seller's ProfitWell dashboard as 'proof' of their churn numbers. The dashboard is only as accurate as the inputs and configuration underneath it — and those are controlled by the seller. ChurnLens's value is that it works from the raw ledger independently, so the verification is not dependent on the seller's tool configuration.

Key terms, defined

Revenue concentration
The share of total revenue coming from the largest customers — high concentration is a churn and valuation risk.
Logo retention
The percentage of customers (logos) retained over a period, independent of expansion revenue.
Net revenue retention (NRR)
Revenue retained from existing customers including expansion and contraction, expressed as a percentage.
