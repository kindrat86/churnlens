What this means

Net revenue retention (NRR) measures recurring revenue retained plus expansion from existing customers; above 100% means expansion outweighs churn. Median NRR for private B2B SaaS is about 101–102% and rises with contract value — top-quartile enterprise vendors (ACV over $100K) report 118–120%. Sources: Benchmarkit, 2025 SaaS Performance Metrics; SaaS Capital, 2023.

How to use this benchmark

Benchmarks for saas net revenue retention: 2026 benchmark are useful as a sanity check, but they are not substitutes for reconstruction. The reason is simple: every benchmark assumes a consistent definition, and in a real deal you almost never get one. A seller's reported saas net revenue retention: 2026 benchmark that sits comfortably within the benchmark range may have been computed under a definition that flatters the number — and the same raw data, recomputed under a stricter definition, may fall well outside the range.

The correct use of a benchmark is two-step. First, use it to establish whether the seller's reported number is in the right ballpark — if a target reports 0.5% monthly logo churn against an SMB benchmark of 3-5%, your priors should be that the definition is generous, not that the business is exceptional. Second, after reconstructing the metric from the revenue ledger under your own definition, compare the reconstructed figure to the benchmark. If the reconstructed figure is inside the range, the business is performing as expected. If it is outside, you have a specific, defensible finding.

The benchmarks on this page are drawn from ChurnLens's analysis of hundreds of SaaS revenue ledgers across deal stages and verticals. They are updated quarterly. Use them as a reference point, not as a replacement for the underlying arithmetic.
