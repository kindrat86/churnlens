Annual churn & retention by contract value

For B2B SaaS, annual revenue retention is the metric acquirers care about. It improves steadily with deal size — bigger contracts churn less and expand more.

Annual contract value (ACV)Median gross revenue retention≈ Annual gross revenue churnMedian net revenue retention
Under $12K90%~10%100%
$12K–$25K90%~10%102%
$25K–$50K92%~8%103%
$50K–$100K93%~7%105%
$100K–$250K93%~7%107%
Over $250K93%~7%110%

Source: SaaS Capital, 2023 B2B SaaS Retention Benchmarks (~1,500 private B2B SaaS companies). Overall median NRR corroborated at 101% by Benchmarkit's 2025 report (FY2024); top-quartile enterprise (ACV > $100K) reaches 118–120%.

Monthly subscriber churn by segment

Recurly publishes average monthly subscriber (logo) churn. Its base skews toward self-serve and consumer subscriptions, so these run far higher than annual enterprise-B2B logo churn — read them as monthly, not annual, and not as a B2B benchmark.

SegmentAverage monthly subscriber churn
Overall (all industries)3.27%
Software & business/professional services3.8%
Consumer / B2C (media, retail, education)6.5%

Source: Recurly, Churn Rate Benchmarks by Industry. Monthly, subscription-billing base — not directly comparable to annual B2B logo churn.

Reading these together: logo churn and revenue churn tell different stories. A company with low logo churn but high revenue churn has a concentration problem; net revenue retention below 100% means expansion isn't offsetting churn — a red flag for acquirers. Longer contract terms and higher ACV both correlate with better retention.
