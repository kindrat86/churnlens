About Churn Lens

Churn Lens (churnlens.site) is a buyer-side SaaS due-diligence tool. When you're about to acquire a SaaS business, you upload the target's raw subscription data (MRR, plan, and logo-level records as CSVs) and Churn Lens returns a buyer-side risk report in minutes — so you price the revenue that's actually there, not the revenue the seller's P&L claims.

Why we built this

Churn Lens started with a specific, expensive kind of surprise: a seller reports 2% monthly churn, the raw CSV says 9.4%, and that gap quietly costs the buyer six figures after close. Sellers don't volunteer the downgrades excluded from their churn math, the annual customers who already cancelled, or the inactive accounts still counted as MRR. Generic subscription-analytics dashboards are built for operators watching their own metrics — not for a buyer stress-testing someone else's numbers under a deadline. Churn Lens is built for the buyer's side of the table.

What it does

Upload the target's subscription CSV and Churn Lens runs six analyses, ranked by how much each threatens the deal:

  • Churn Divergence Detector — reported churn vs. what the raw data actually shows.
  • Concentration Vulnerability Index — how exposed revenue is to a handful of accounts.
  • Annual-Plan Decay Projection — how annual-plan revenue erodes as cohorts roll off.
  • Zombie MRR Detector — inactive paid accounts inflating the headline MRR.
  • Revenue Quality Scorecard — a benchmarked score for how durable the revenue is.
  • MRR Trajectory Forensics — whether growth is organic or engineered for sale.

The output is a benchmarked revenue-quality score plus a ranked red-flag report — self-serve, in minutes, no data-science team required.

Who it's for

SaaS acquirers and searchers, private-equity and M&A analysts, brokers, and investors running due diligence on a SaaS target before purchase. Start free, run a single deal for a one-off fee, or subscribe for ongoing diligence.

The name

Churn Lens is an independent product at churnlens.site, focused specifically on buyer-side acquisition due diligence. It isn't affiliated with other similarly named tools — our lane is the buyer's side of a SaaS deal.
