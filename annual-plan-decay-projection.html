The Annual-Plan Decay Projection

Identifies customers locked into annual plans who are statistically likely to cancel at renewal. This is the quietest revenue killer in SaaS M&A — invisible on the P&L until renewal season hits after close, by which point the acquirer owns the problem.

What it is

Identifies customers locked into annual plans who are statistically likely to cancel at renewal. This is the quietest revenue killer in SaaS M&A — invisible on the P&L until renewal season hits after close, by which point the acquirer owns the problem.

What it looks for in the data

The projection isolates annual-plan customers, measures their remaining contract lifetime, and overlays behavioral signals (usage, support tickets, payment cadence) that historically precede renewal cancellation. The output is a projected renewal-rate curve by cohort.

Buyer-side signal: this lens surfaces a risk that sellers rarely volunteer because it makes their headline number look worse. In a SaaS acquisition, the gap between what this lens finds and what the CIM discloses is directly negotiation-relevant.
