Stripe Sigma Review for SaaS Acquirers: Raw Payment Data vs. Revenue Quality Analysis [2026]

A review of Stripe Sigma from the perspective of a SaaS acquirer — what its payment data gives you and what it cannot tell you about revenue durability.

Stripe Sigma is Stripe's data-querying tool that lets you run custom SQL on your Stripe transaction data. For an acquirer who can get a seller to share Sigma-level access, it offers a raw, unfiltered view of payment activity. But raw payment data is not the same as a revenue-quality assessment — and confusing the two is one of the most common diligence mistakes.

What Stripe Sigma does well

  • Raw transaction data — every charge, refund, subscription event, and invoice in SQL-queryable form
  • Custom queries — build any report you can imagine from the Stripe data schema
  • Revenue charting — MRR, churn, and subscription trends from the source
  • Data export — pull any query result into CSV for further analysis

What Stripe Sigma does not give acquirers

Our honest assessment

Stripe Sigma is a powerful raw-data tool — if you know exactly what queries to run and how to interpret the results. The gap is that most acquirers do not know which queries map to deal-killing risks (concentration, cliff, zombie MRR) without a framework. Sigma gives you the ingredients; ChurnLens gives you the recipe and the finished dish.

Best practice: Ask the seller for Stripe Sigma read-only access to validate the raw data. Then run ChurnLens over the same data to get the structured revenue-quality assessment. Sigma for data integrity verification; ChurnLens for risk analysis and deal-memo output.

Verdict

Stripe Sigma is excellent for data verification and custom analysis if you have the SQL skills. For deal-ready revenue quality scoring with benchmarking, concentration analysis, and structured output, ChurnLens provides what Sigma leaves as an exercise for the analyst.
