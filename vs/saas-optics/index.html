Churn Lens vs SaaSOptics

An honest comparison. SaaSOptics is a billing platform without pre-acquisition churn forensics.

Why choose Churn Lens over SaaSOptics?

SaaSOptics is a billing and financial operations platform (now part of Maxio), built for operators tracking their own business. Churn Lens is built for the other side of the table: buyer-side due diligence on a SaaS you are thinking of acquiring, using nothing but the seller's raw subscription CSV.

What Churn Lens computes from a target's subscription CSV

  • Logo churn and revenue churn, computed separately and compared — the divergence between them is the signal
  • Concentration Vulnerability Index — flags targets where a handful of customers carry an outsized share of MRR
  • Annual-Plan Decay Projection — identifies annual-plan customers statistically likely to cancel at renewal
  • Zombie MRR Detector — finds accounts that keep paying but stopped engaging
  • Revenue Quality Scorecard — a composite A–F grade weighting retention, concentration, expansion, and growth trend
  • MRR Trajectory Forensics — separates genuinely growing MRR from new sales masking declining cohorts

Which tool fits your job?

If you run a SaaS and want billing operations and financial reporting for your own SaaS, an operator-side tool like SaaSOptics is the natural choice. Churn Lens has one job: interrogating someone else's subscription data before an acquisition, when all you have is a CSV export and a CIM you cannot fully trust.

Operators monitor revenue they already know. Acquirers have to stress-test revenue they don't. That is why Churn Lens reports lead with risk — hidden churn, concentration, renewal cliffs — rather than growth dashboards.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Churn Lens and SaaSOptics together?

Yes — they solve different problems at different times. Acquirers typically run Churn Lens on the target's exported subscription CSV during due diligence, before the deal closes. After the acquisition, the operator keeps using the analytics stack the business already has. Churn Lens reads a one-off CSV export, so it requires no integration with SaaSOptics or any other tooling.

How much does a Churn Lens analysis cost?

The Starter tier is free and covers one CSV analysis per month with logo churn, revenue churn, concentration risk, and a revenue quality score. A single full Pro report costs $9 one-time. Ongoing acquirers use Pro at $49 per month; M&A advisors running many deals use Dealmaker at $199 per month.
