ChurnLens vs Paddle

Paddle is a payment and revenue platform — it tracks MRR, subscriptions, and revenue analytics for operating businesses. ChurnLens is a buyer-side due diligence tool — it analyzes seller subscription data to find hidden churn, concentration risk, and revenue decay before acquisition. They serve different stages: Paddle helps you run the business; ChurnLens helps you buy one.

Key differences

  • Paddle: merchant-of-record + revenue ops for operators
  • ChurnLens: buyer-side churn forensics for acquirers
  • Paddle tracks current-state revenue; ChurnLens models post-close decay
  • ChurnLens scores revenue quality on 5 dimensions Paddle doesn't measure
