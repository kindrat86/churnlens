When to choose each: ChurnLens vs Subscript

The choice between ChurnLens and Subscript is not either/or — it depends entirely on which side of the deal you are on and what question you are trying to answer. Subscript is an operator-facing tool built for someone running a subscription business who needs to track their own metrics from a live billing integration. ChurnLens is a buyer-side tool built for someone evaluating a subscription business they do not yet own, working from a data-room export rather than a live integration. The two tools are designed for different stages of the same deal lifecycle.

If you are acquiring a SaaS business, the typical workflow is: run ChurnLens first on the seller's revenue-ledger CSV to establish ground truth and identify the gaps between reported and reconstructed metrics. Then, post-close, evaluate whether to keep Subscript (if the target was already using it) or migrate to whatever metrics platform fits your portfolio standard. The pre-close verification is the step that protects the purchase price; the post-close platform choice is operational housekeeping.

  • Use Subscript when: You own the business, have live billing access, and need ongoing operational dashboards for the team running it day-to-day.
  • Use ChurnLens when: You are evaluating a business you do not yet own, have a data-room CSV but no live integration, and need to verify whether the seller's reported churn and retention numbers are accurate before you commit capital.
  • Use both when: You are a serial acquirer who wants a standardized pre-close diligence layer (ChurnLens) across every deal, plus a standardized post-close metrics platform (Subscript) across every portfolio company.
