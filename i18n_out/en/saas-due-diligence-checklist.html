SaaS Due Diligence Checklist

19 critical checks before you buy a SaaS business. Organized by risk domain — churn, concentration, revenue quality, unit economics, technical debt, and legal.

Every SaaS acquisition looks good in the CIM. The deal memo hides the landmines. This checklist forces you to look at each one — before you sign.

1 – Churn & Retention Risk

CRITICAL Gross Monthly Logo Churn

What percentage of customers cancel each month? Benchmark against sector medians. Anything above 5% monthly churn for B2B SaaS requires a structural reason (clear intent, seasonal business). Ask for the raw data, not a calculated metric.

CRITICAL Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

NRR = (Starting MRR + Expansion – Churn) / Starting MRR. An NRR >100% means expansion offsets churn — the asset grows itself. Below 85% means the company is slowly dying. Demand the calculation method.

CRITICAL Cohort Retention Curves

A single churn number hides cohort decay. Newer cohorts that churn faster than old ones indicate product-market fit erosion, competitive pressure, or a degrading onboarding experience. Request cohort tables by quarter of acquisition.

HIGH Voluntary vs. Involuntary Churn Split

Involuntary churn (failed payments, expired cards) is fixable. Voluntary churn (cancelled because the product wasn't valuable enough) is structural. If voluntary churn exceeds 60% of total, the value prop needs work — and you'll inherit that work.

2 – Concentration Risk

CRITICAL Customer Concentration (Top 5 / Top 10)

If the top 5 customers represent more than 30% of MRR, you're buying a services business with a SaaS wrapper — not a product company. Losing one customer permanently changes the valuation basis. Calculate it yourself from raw CSV data.

CRITICAL Revenue Concentration by Plan Tier

If 70% of MRR comes from "Enterprise" tier customers, the revenue base is fragile during an acquisition transition. Enterprise customers churn less but churn harder when they do (more revenue per lost logo).

HIGH Segment / Geo Concentration

Is revenue concentrated in one vertical or geography? A single GDPR regulatory shift, currency fluctuation, or sector downturn can wipe 40%+ of MRR overnight.

3 – Revenue Quality & Decay

CRITICAL MRR Trend (Trailing 12 Months)

Flat or declining MRR over 12 months is the #1 deal-killer signal we see in Churn Lens analyses. Ask for month-by-month MRR for the last 24 months and look for inflection points (price changes, product launches, churn waves).

HIGH Contraction Rate

Expansion revenue sounds good until you realize it's masking customers downgrading plans. Contraction (downgrades + plan reductions) tells you if the core value prop is eroding. Request downgrade events separately from churn.

HIGH Payment Velocity (Days-to-Churn)

The average time between signup and first payment tells you whether the product delivers quick value. Long payment velocity combined with high churn is a double signal: the product doesn't hook users fast enough.

4 – Unit Economics

HIGH LTV / CAC Ratio

A 3:1 LTV/CAC is the SaaS baseline. Below 3:1 means the company loses money on each customer over its lifetime. Above 5:1 means they may be under-investing in growth. Calculate from raw data — don't accept the seller's blended number.

HIGH CAC Payback Period

How many months of gross margin does it take to recover customer acquisition costs? Over 18 months is risky for a bootstrapped acquisition. Under 6 months and the pricing may be too low.

MEDIUM Gross Margin Trend

A declining gross margin trend signals infrastructure bloat, rising cloud costs, or increasing support load per customer. Stable or improving margins are a green flag.

5 – Technical & Operational Risk

HIGH Codebase / Architecture Debt

Check the dependency tree age, upgrade cadence, and whether there's automated testing. A stovepiped codebase with no CI/CD and a single developer key-man risk can add 6–12 months to your post-acquisition integration plan.

MEDIUM Single Point of Failure (Personnel)

Is the product running because one person knows the infrastructure? A founder-run micro-SaaS that's never been documented will crater within 90 days of the founder leaving. Look at commit authorship distribution.

MEDIUM Cloud / Infrastructure Lock-in

A serverless app tightly coupled to a single cloud provider's services is hard to migrate. Factor migration costs into your valuation model.

6 – Legal & Compliance Risk

HIGH Terms of Service / Privacy Compliance

GDPR, CCPA, and sector-specific regulations change ownership obligations if you acquire. Check whether the current ToS allows assignment, and whether data processing agreements are in place with every customer.

MEDIUM Intellectual Property Ownership

Contractors without signed IP assignments, open-source license violations in the codebase, or trademark gaps in key markets can all surface post-close. Verify all contributor agreements.

MEDIUM Vendor / Subprocessor Dependencies

Every SaaS the business relies on (Stripe, AWS, SendGrid, OpenAI) is a risk if the plan is unsignable. Check change-of-control clauses in vendor contracts.
