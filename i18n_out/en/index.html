The 5-Risk Buyer-Side Method

Every risk a seller can hide — structured into a named framework. Not tips. A system.

01

The Churn Divergence Detector

Logo churn and revenue churn, computed separately and compared. Losing 5% of logos is fine. Losing 5% of logos representing 40% of MRR is a dealbreaker. The divergence IS the signal.

02

The Concentration Vulnerability Index

Flags when 3 customers represent 60%+ of MRR. If any of those churn post-close, your "SaaS" becomes a professional services firm overnight. Index scored, not just flagged.

03

The Annual-Plan Decay Projection

Identifies customers locked into annual plans who are statistically likely to cancel at renewal. The quietest revenue killer — invisible until renewal season hits post-close.

04

The Zombie MRR Detector

Detects customers still paying but not engaging. Zombie MRR looks stable on the P&L but disappears one invoice at a time. The seller never flags this — it makes their number look good.

05

The Revenue Quality Scorecard

A composite A–F grade weighting retention, concentration, expansion revenue, and growth trends. One letter grade tells you more than a 50-page CIM about whether this MRR will still be here in 12 months.

06

The MRR Trajectory Forensics

Shows whether MRR is trending up, flat, or silently decaying. A flat headline MRR often hides declining cohorts being masked by new sales. We separate the two so you see what's real.

The 3 false beliefs that cost buyers $340K

If you're evaluating a SaaS right now, you probably hold at least one of these. Here's why each one is wrong.

FALSE BELIEF #1 — The Vehicle
"The seller's churn methodology is industry-standard, so the number is reliable."
Truth: There is no standard. Every seller makes methodology choices that flatter their number. The only reliable number is the one you compute from raw data. (4.2x average gap between reported and real.)
FALSE BELIEF #2 — Internal Belief
"I'm not technical enough to analyze raw subscription data myself."
Truth: You don't need to be. You need the CSV and a tool that computes churn five ways. The analysis takes minutes, not a finance degree. Your "I'm not technical" belief is exactly what sellers count on.
FALSE BELIEF #3 — External Belief
"The seller wouldn't hide churn — they'd get caught in the earnout."
Truth: Most churn methodology choices are legal, defensible, and invisible. Earnouts don't protect you from legitimate-looking accounting that understates real churn by 4x. The earnout catches fraud, not methodology.
