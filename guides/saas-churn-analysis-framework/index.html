A systematic framework for finding the churn the seller isn't reporting. Cohort analysis, inactive-account detection, and the MRR waterfall that reveals what top-line metrics hide.

The churn that sellers don't show you

Most SaaS sellers report a single churn rate — usually the best-looking one. But churn has multiple faces: voluntary churn (customers who cancel), involuntary churn (failed payments), contraction churn (downgrades), and silent churn (inactive accounts that haven't cancelled yet). A business reporting "3% monthly churn" might actually be losing 5–7% when you include the silent and involuntary layers.

The MRR Waterfall Framework

Instead of looking at a single churn number, build an MRR waterfall: start with beginning-of-month MRR, add new MRR, add expansion MRR, subtract voluntary churn, subtract involuntary churn, subtract contraction, subtract pending-cancellation MRR — and only then do you see the real net change. Most seller-provided reports stop after "add expansion MRR."

Cohort analysis for acquisition due diligence

The most revealing analysis: group customers by signup cohort (e.g., Q1 2025, Q2 2025) and track retention by cohort over time. If newer cohorts are retaining worse than older ones, the product is getting worse or the market is shifting — and your acquisition is buying into a declining trend. Sellers almost never volunteer cohort data.
