Why logistics SaaS churn is different

Logistics and supply-chain saas exhibits a distinct churn signature that generic SaaS benchmarks misread. The dominant pattern is shipper-concentration risk — one carrier loss can gut 20% of MRR. Freight forwarders and 3pls are the typical buyer profile, and their churn behavior is shaped by industry-specific triggers: 55% of revenue sits on annual plans (so reported churn lags real decay by a full renewal cycle), and MRR volatility is high (so a single quarter's snapshot can flatter or slander the true trajectory).

The result is that logistics SaaS sellers can present clean-looking retention slides — gross revenue churn "under 3.0%", logo retention "above 95%" — while the underlying business is hollowing out. ChurnLens is built to find the hollowing.

The decay signals ChurnLens tracks for logistics SaaS

  • Revenue concentration: what share of MRR sits in the top-5 logos? For logistics and supply-chain SaaS, concentration above 35% is a structural risk that surfaces as sudden, lumpy churn.
  • Inactive paid accounts: paying customers (freight forwarders and 3PLs) whose product usage has dropped below the activation threshold. These are logos that have already churned in behavior — they just haven't churned in billing yet.
  • Annual-plan renewal risk: with 55% annual share, ChurnLens projects which cohorts are scheduled to renew in the next 1–4 quarters and models the downside case.
  • MRR decline patterns: high MRR volatility means ChurnLens looks at trailing 6-month weighted averages, not the most recent month.
  • Cohort retention curves: whether the 4.5% logo churn is concentrated in old cohorts (a maturing product problem) or new cohorts (an onboarding/ICP problem).
