Common diligence scenarios for Churn due diligence for SaaS acquirers

Churn due diligence for SaaS acquirers typically encounter ChurnLens at one of three moments: evaluating a specific SaaS target before committing capital, preparing a SaaS business for sale and wanting to anticipate what a buyer's diligence will find, or monitoring a portfolio of SaaS positions for early warning signs of revenue-quality decay. In all three cases the underlying analysis is identical — reconstruct churn and retention from the revenue ledger, flag zombie MRR and renewal cliffs, stress-test revenue concentration — but the deliverable and the urgency differ.

For churn due diligence for saas acquirers, the highest-impact use is the pre-commitment verification. The seller's data room contains a churn number; that number is, in our experience, understated by an average of 4.2× once you reconstruct it from the raw ledger under a consistent definition. The gap between those two numbers — reported vs reconstructed — maps directly to dollars of purchase price. Every dollar of verified overstatement is a dollar you can defend reducing, and the reconstruction gives you the specific customer-level evidence to make that defense stick.

The second scenario is preparation. If you are a founder or operator planning to sell, running ChurnLens on your own business first tells you exactly what a buyer will find, gives you months to fix or contextualize the problems, and lets you arrive at diligence with a clean report and explanations rather than surprises. Founders who pre-audit consistently command higher multiples than founders who get blindsided by their own numbers.
